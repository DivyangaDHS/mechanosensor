The primary objective of this phase was to establish a strong theoretical foundation for the project titled "Cancellation of Self-Sensing due to Ego-Motion of Vibration-Based Mechanosensors" . During these two weeks, existing research related to bio-inspired tactile sensing, vibration-based robotic antennae, ego-motion cancellation, and machine learning approaches for sensor signal prediction was studied in detail.

Activities Performed
  • Conducted an extensive literature survey on vibration-based mechanosensors and tactile sensing systems.
  • Studied biological sensing mechanisms used by insects, particularly antenna-based exploration and contact detection.
  • Reviewed the effects of ego-motion on vibration-based sensors and the challenges it introduces to contact detection.
  • Investigated machine learning techniques used for predicting and filtering self-generated sensor signals.
  • Analyzed previous implementations of robotic antenna systems and tactile navigation platforms.
Key Findings

The literature review revealed that self-generated vibrations caused by robot motion can significantly affect the accuracy of contact detection systems. Previous studies demonstrated that Echo State Networks (ESNs) are highly effective in predicting expected sensor signals generated by robot motion. By comparing the predicted signal with the measured sensor signal, genuine contact events can be isolated with high accuracy.

"The literature review confirmed that an ESN-based approach is a promising solution for cancelling ego-motion effects in vibration-based tactile sensors."
